Biography

Of the late Emmanuel Offei Koram(a.k.a. Ackork)

Emmanuel Offei Koram (affectionately called Papa Offei or Ackork by friends and family) was born on 25 September 1927 to David Yaw Koram and Felicia Ataa Asiedua; the last of their six children.

Education

He had his primary education at the Mamfe Presbyterian Primary School, continuing to Akropong Senior School (Salem), where he made some lifelong friends. After that, Emmanuel went to Accra Academy Secondary School where he obtained the Cambridge School Leaving Certificate in 1948 and the London Matriculation Certificate in 1949.
From 1953 to 1954 he attended the Ballam and Tooting College of Commerce in London, where he successfully passed the Intermediate Examination of Association of International Accountants in June 1954.
He performed his articleship under James Magee & Co, a firm of Chartered Accountants in London from 1955 to 1960. During this time, he studied accountancy and passed his final exams for the Institute of Chartered Accountants, England and Wales, attaining membership in 1960 and was made a fellow of the Institute ten years later in 1970.

Work Experience

Before heading off to the UK to start his accountancy course, Emmanuel taught at the Suhum Presbyterian Senior School from January 1949 to March 1950. This was followed by a spell at HM Customs and Excise where he rose from an Officer to Executive Officer over the course of a few years from April 1950 to September 1953.
After returning from London, Emmanuel joined the Cocoa Marketing Company Ghana Limited in 1960 as their Chief Accountant in October 1960 and remained with them until December 1975.
Following this, he was the Managing Director of the Cocoa Processing Company from 1977 to 1978 before becoming the Director of Finance at the Cocoa Marketing Board (COCOBOD).
In February 1980, Emmanuel joined the Presbyterian Church of Ghana as the Treasurer (this position is now known as Director of Finance) and served the church in that position till December 1988. Additionally, he was the Board Chairman for the Presbyterian Book Depot for some years during his period with the church.
From 1989 to 2001, Emmanuel was a Financial Consultant at Vanguard Assurance Company Limited, before successfully running his own accountancy firm (Emmanuel Koram & Associates) for many years. During this period he supported numerous clients from a variety of industries with their financial affairs until his retirement in 2012.

Family Life

Emmanuel Koram married the late Abena Amponsah in May 1975 and they were blessed with two daughters, Afua Aseidua and Akua Amponsaa, who were his pride and joy.
He was a great unifier and a wonderful brother, uncle and grandfather to all who knew him and always gladly participated in all family events throughout his life.
As a dedicated follower of Christ, he made it a point to introduce anyone who stayed with him to Christianity and showed affection, love and compassion to everyone and at all times, irrespective of one’s creed, tribe, gender or age.
He loved the Lord and few days before his passing asked one of his nieces if she knew of any relatives who did not know the Lord so that he would speak to them about Jesus. He told her tell any such person that Jesus is alive.
Generous to a fault with his time, wisdom and financially; nothing was a problem to Papa Offei as he made everyone feel at home.

Other activities

Papa Offei (who became affectionately known as Ackork (Akokora) a nickname given to him by his daughters) was extremely witty. The jokes came so readily to him that people said he was a Fante ‘concert’ man from Mamfe. He was always a joy to be with and both children and adults loved being in his company.
His constant positive spirit and focus on God helped him navigate life’s challenges and he advised others to do the same 6 In Loving Memory of EMMANUEL O. KORAM when they encountered difficulties in their lives.
He took great delight in worshipping God through his love of music and was a gifted musician who played the flute during his school years before learning the guitar, double bass, and harmonica later in his life. Fortunately, he was able to play his harmonica until his dying day.
As a skillful driver and patient teacher, he taught numerous people how to drive by providing encouragement and help, especially to those who were fearful about getting behind the wheel again after an accident.
His willingness to serve others also led him the Rotary Club where he followed their motto ”Service above self” all the days of his life.
His love of photography enabled him to capture hundreds of pictures of friends and family that created treasured memories for many, especially in the days before smart phones.
His passion for his career and desire to help others attain knowledge led him to mentor and train several trainee accountants as well as support the Institute of Chartered Accountant, Ghana as a tutor and an examiner.

Christian Life

Emmanuel Koram was a member of the Hour of Visitation Choir and Evangelistic Association (HOVCEA) and accompanied the choir with his double bass for many years.
Papa Koram worshiped the Kaneshie Presbyterian Church from the early 1970s and attended church services religiously till the end. Even when he was weak and frail, he insisted on going to church to worship his maker. We remember with delight and thankfulness to God all his dance moves while the singing band sang “Me ne Nyame n’ɛnam ɔka me ho nti me nsuro” during the last church service he attended on the 19th March 2023.
He served at Kaneshie Presbyterian Church in various roles including patron of the Youth Choir, Patron of the Singing Band and a member of the Bible Study and Prayer Group. He was Head of Finance of the Congregation at the church for many years and the Congregation recognized him by naming the Finance Office after him (now known as “E. O. Koram Office”).
Additionally, he was a life long supporter of the Presbyterian Church of Ghana in Mamfe (his hometown), which the members appreciated and several even visited him a few days before he was taken ill and passed away on the 5th April 2023.
We celebrate his godly life and love of service which will live on for generations to come. We acknowledge his great works in our lives. We give thanks for his long and fruitful life. We take solace in his relationship with the Lord and the fact that he is resting peacefully in God’s everlasting majesty until we meet again.
